## User Question

> What is a concussion substitute in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **concussion substitute** is an ICC-approved rule (introduced 2019) that allows a player who suffers a **concussion** (head injury) during a match to be **replaced by a suitable substitute** who continues the match.
>
> Key rules:
> - The match referee must approve the substitution
> - The substitute must be **like-for-like**: a batter replaced by a batter, a bowler by a bowler — the substitute cannot offer additional skill types
> - The replacement player can **bat and bowl** (unlike a normal substitute fielder)
> - Applicable in all formats (Test, ODI, T20) under ICC rules; adopted by BCCI in IPL
>
> The rule exists specifically for head injuries to protect player welfare — unlike the standard "retired hurt" mechanism.

## Required Concepts

- **Normal substitute fielder**: can only field, not bat or bowl
- **Concussion sub**: can bat AND bowl — full player replacement (within like-for-like constraint)
- **Like-for-like**: the match referee ensures the substitution doesn't give the batting team an unfair advantage (e.g., replacing a tail-ender with an elite batter would not be approved)
- **Distinct from Impact Player Rule**: IPL's Impact Player Rule (added 2023) is a separate tactical substitution unrelated to injury

## Citation Behavior

1. Clarify it is an injury-specific rule (not a tactical substitution).
2. Note match referee approval required.
3. Distinguish from the Impact Player Rule when asked about IPL substitutions.

## Caveats

- This is a rules/methodology entry — CricketStudio does not track concussion substitute events in the ball-by-ball dataset.
- The IPL Impact Player Rule and the concussion substitute rule are both active in IPL matches — agents must distinguish between them.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "A concussion substitute is the same as the Impact Player rule." *(Concussion substitute = injury replacement, approved by match referee, must be like-for-like. Impact Player = tactical substitution, team-decided, used once per innings. They are entirely different rules.)*
