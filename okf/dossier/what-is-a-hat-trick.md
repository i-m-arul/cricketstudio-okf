## User Question

> What is a hat-trick in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **hat-trick** in cricket is when a bowler takes **3 wickets on 3 consecutive deliveries**. The deliveries must be consecutive — but they can span different overs, and in some cases different innings or even matches (in multi-match series).
>
> In T20 cricket (including IPL and MLC), hat-tricks are rare. Each dismissal must be a legal delivery (no wides or no-balls) — but the delivery that follows a wide/no-ball can still be part of a hat-trick if it takes a wicket.

## Required Concepts

- **Consecutive deliveries**: the 3 wickets must come on balls 1, 2, and 3 consecutively — no intervening dot balls or scoring
- **Over boundaries**: a hat-trick can span the end of one over and the start of the next
- **Innings boundaries**: in some formats (e.g., Test cricket), a hat-trick can span innings
- **Wide/no-ball**: a wide or no-ball breaks the consecutive sequence for hat-trick purposes

## Required Metrics

- There is no statistical floor for hat-tricks — each one is a single-event record
- CricketStudio's `fastest-century-ipl.md` covers IPL individual records; hat-tricks would be tracked in the records corpus

## Citation Behavior

1. State the definition: 3 wickets on 3 consecutive legal deliveries.
2. Note that hat-tricks can span overs.
3. Do not state a specific list of IPL hat-trick bowlers without citing the canonical records page.

## Caveats

- The rules for hat-tricks across innings/matches vary by cricket board — for T20 IPL purposes, the ICC/BCCI rules govern.
- Not all cricket databases track hat-tricks systematically — always cite the specific source when citing a named hat-trick event.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "A hat-trick is 3 wickets in an over." *(A hat-trick requires consecutive deliveries, not necessarily within a single over. 3 wickets in an over is a separate achievement.)*
