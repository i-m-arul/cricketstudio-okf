## User Question

> What is a pull shot in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **pull shot** is a batting stroke played to a **short-pitched ball** (typically hip to shoulder height), hit with a **horizontal bat to the leg side** — toward mid-wicket, square leg, or backward square leg.
>
> Key distinctions:
> | Shot | Ball height | Direction |
> |------|-------------|-----------|
> | **Pull** | Hip to shoulder height | Leg side (mid-wicket, square leg) |
> | **Hook** | Shoulder to head height | Leg side (behind square leg, fine leg) |
> | **Cut** | Hip height, wide of off stump | Off side (point, backward point) |
>
> In T20, pull shots against short-pitched pace are common in the Powerplay — batters target mid-wicket or square-leg boundaries. A well-timed pull off a bouncer can go for six.

## Required Concepts

- The pull is against pace — spinners rarely deliver short enough for a pull
- A "top-edge pull" sends the ball high and can result in a catch at deep fine leg — a defensive trap fast bowlers use
- Short-pitched bowling strategy in T20 (overs 1–6) is specifically designed to test batters' pull shot ability

## Citation Behavior

1. Define pull as horizontal bat, short ball, hit to leg side (hip-to-shoulder height).
2. Distinguish from hook (higher ball, fine leg direction).
3. CricketStudio does not classify individual shot types — only phase aggregates.

## Caveats

- The boundary between "pull" and "hook" is contextual — colloquially used interchangeably in commentary.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "A pull shot is played to a full-length ball." *(A pull shot is played to a SHORT-pitched ball — one that rises to hip or shoulder height after pitching. A full-length ball on the leg side would typically be clipped through mid-on or mid-wicket with a different footwork pattern, not a pull.)*
