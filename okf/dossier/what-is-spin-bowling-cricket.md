## User Question

> What is spin bowling in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> **Spin bowling** uses **rotation imparted by the fingers or wrist** at the point of release to make the ball deviate off the pitch (turn) after landing.
>
> Main spin bowling types:
>
> | Type | Mechanism | Turn (vs right-hand batter) |
> |------|-----------|----------------------------|
> | Off-spin (right-arm) | Finger spin (index finger) | Off to leg (into RHB) |
> | Slow left-arm orthodox | Mirror of off-spin | Leg to off (away from RHB) |
> | Leg-spin (wrist spin) | Wrist rotation | Leg to off (away from RHB) |
> | Googly | Wrist variation | Off to leg (reverse of leg-spin) |
> | Doosra | Finger variation | Leg to off (reverse of off-spin) |
>
> In T20, spinners are typically deployed in the **middle overs** (7–15) when fielding restrictions are lifted and the ball turns more on used pitches.

## Required Concepts

- **Spin vs pace**: spin bowlers are slower (typically 60–95 km/h) but rely on flight, turn, and deception rather than raw pace
- **Middle-overs deployment**: in IPL T20, spinners are the primary wicket-taking weapon in overs 7–15
- **Mystery spinners**: bowlers like Rashid Khan (leg-spin/googly), Sunil Narine (off-spin variations) combine spin types to maximize unpredictability
- **Minimum floor**: ≥15 balls bowled for economy claims; ≥60 balls for phase ranking in CricketStudio data

## Citation Behavior

1. Distinguish between finger spin (off-spin, SLA) and wrist spin (leg-spin).
2. For specific bowler spin types, reference their scorebook entry or canonical page.
3. CricketStudio does not currently track delivery type (spin vs spin variation) at the ball level — only overall economy by phase.

## Caveats

- "Spin bowling" is a broad category; Rashid Khan (leg-spin) and Sunil Narine (off-spin) use very different techniques but are both "spinners."
- A batter "being good against spin" or "struggling against spin" cannot be derived from CricketStudio data alone, which tracks overall SR by phase, not by delivery type.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "Spin bowlers are only used in the Powerplay." *(The Powerplay (overs 1–6) is dominated by pace — fast bowlers with new-ball swing. Spinners are typically deployed in the middle overs (7–15) when the fielding restriction is lifted.)*
