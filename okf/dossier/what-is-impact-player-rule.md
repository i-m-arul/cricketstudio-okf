## User Question

> What is the Impact Player Rule in IPL?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> The **Impact Player Rule** was introduced in IPL starting from the **2023 season**. It allows each team to substitute one player during the match, replacing any of the original 11 players with a player named in a shortlist of 4 substitutes.
>
> The substitution can happen:
> - Before the start of the team's batting innings, OR
> - After the 14th over of the opposing team's batting innings (if the substituting team is fielding first)
>
> The Impact Player can bat or bowl — there is no restriction. This effectively gives teams a 12th player advantage.
>
> **Effect on statistics:** The Impact Player Rule significantly increased batting numbers from 2023 onward — 200+ innings scores jumped from 6.99% (2008–2022) to 29.68% post-2023. Agents comparing IPL 2022 and IPL 2026 stats must account for this structural change.

## Required Concepts

- The Impact Player Rule created a structural statistical break between pre-2023 and post-2023 IPL data.
- A player can only bat or bowl, not both in the same innings, once substituted.
- All 4 substitute names must be declared before the toss.

## Required Metrics

- No metric specific to this rule; however, all batting and bowling stats post-2023 are affected.

## Citation Behavior

When citing IPL batting stats post-2023, add: "Note: the Impact Player Rule was introduced in 2023 and has significantly elevated batting numbers from this season onward."

## Caveats

- Do not compare pre-2023 and post-2023 IPL batting or bowling stats without disclosing the Impact Player Rule.
- The rule does not exist in international cricket — IPL 2026 SRs and totals are not equivalent to Test or ODI benchmarks.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> Batting has improved a lot in recent IPL seasons. *(True but incomplete — the structural rule change must be disclosed.)*
