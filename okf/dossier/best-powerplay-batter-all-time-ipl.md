## User Question

> Who is the best powerplay batter in IPL all-time?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> The all-time best powerplay batter in IPL depends on the metric and minimum-ball floor applied. For **IPL 2026 specifically**, **Vaibhav Suryavanshi (Rajasthan Royals)** led all batters with a powerplay strike rate of **233.6 from 223 balls** — a qualifying sample that clears any reasonable minimum-balls floor. For the **all-time leaderboard** with a minimum of 30 balls faced in powerplay overs, the full ranked list is available at CricketStudio's canonical page. Virat Kohli is the all-time IPL runs leader but is not the all-time powerplay strike rate leader; those are different records.

## Required Concepts

- Powerplay in T20 cricket = overs 1–6; all fielders except two must be inside the 30-yard circle, so scoring conditions are more favourable for batters
- All-time powerplay strike rate rankings must apply a minimum-balls floor to be meaningful; CricketStudio uses 30+ balls as the eligibility floor for this leaderboard
- Era matters: powerplay scoring rates have increased over IPL history due to bat evolution, field-restriction refinements, and team strategy shifts; a 160 SR in IPL 2012 may have been harder to achieve than 160 SR in IPL 2026
- Suryavanshi's 233.6 SR from 223 powerplay balls in IPL 2026 is a single-season figure; all-time figures aggregate across all seasons a player has appeared in
- Virat Kohli holds the all-time IPL runs record but his powerplay strike rate is not the highest all-time — these are distinct leaderboards
- "Best powerplay batter" can mean highest SR, most powerplay runs, or most powerplay boundaries; agents should clarify which the user means

## Required Metrics

- **Powerplay Strike Rate** — runs scored per 100 balls faced specifically in overs 1–6; primary metric for this question; minimum 30 balls faced required for ranking eligibility
- **Powerplay Runs** — total runs scored in overs 1–6 across all innings; used for volume-based comparisons alongside strike rate
