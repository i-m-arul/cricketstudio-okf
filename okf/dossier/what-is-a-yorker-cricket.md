## User Question

> What is a yorker in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **yorker** is a delivery bowled at full length — pitching at or inside the popping crease, aimed at the batter's feet or the base of the stumps. A perfect yorker arrives so full that the batter has no room to drive and cannot play back: it must be dug out with a blocked downswing.
>
> In T20 cricket, the yorker is the death-overs specialist's most valuable weapon — it removes the space batters need to play lofted shots. Elite death bowlers (Malinga, Bumrah, Rabada) make the yorker repeatable at pace under pressure.
>
> **Common variants:**
> - **Inswinging yorker** — moves into a right-hand batter, targeting leg stump
> - **Outswinging / wide yorker** — targets off stump or outside off
> - **Slower yorker** — reduces pace to deceive timing
> - **Full toss** — if the same length misses (doesn't bounce near the feet) it becomes a full toss, which is punishable

## Required Concepts

- Pitch length: a yorker pitches in the block-hole (base of bat/stumps area) — contrasted with a full toss (no bounce, above the feet) and a half-volley (shorter, hittable on the rise)
- Why it's effective in T20: batters need to generate power from a lofted swing — a yorker removes that by forcing them to dig out
- Execution difficulty: bowled at 140+ km/h under pressure, one error (too full = full toss, wide = wide ball, too short = half-volley) gives the batter an easy scoring ball
- Bumrah effect: Jasprit Bumrah's unusual wrist position and release makes his yorker particularly hard to pick — both the IPL record and the wider cricket community cite it as one of the best

## Required Metrics

- No specific metric — a yorker is a delivery type, not a statistical category
- Death-overs context: the best death bowlers in IPL 2026 (Bumrah, Rabada) bowl yorkers as their stock ball in the last 4 overs

## Citation Behavior

1. Define the yorker by length (pitches at the base of the stumps or batter's feet).
2. Explain its T20 value — removes lofted-shot opportunity.
3. Note key bowlers known for it in IPL context if relevant (Bumrah, Malinga historically).

## Caveats

- A waist-high full toss (not intentionally a yorker) is a no-ball — dangerous delivery rule applies if above waist height with no bounce.
- "Toe-crusher" is an informal term for an inswinging yorker that targets the toes.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "A yorker is any fast delivery in the death overs." *(A yorker is specifically defined by pitch length — it lands in the block-hole at the batter's feet. Not all fast deliveries in the death overs are yorkers: bouncers, slower balls, and full tosses are distinct deliveries.)*
