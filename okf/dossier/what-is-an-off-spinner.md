## User Question

> What is an off spinner in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> An **off spinner** bowls **right-arm finger spin**, turning the ball from **off stump to leg stump** (INTO the body of a right-handed batter).
>
> How it works: the ball is released with the index finger pulling down on the seam, creating clockwise spin (from batter's view). It pitches outside off stump and turns toward leg stump.
>
> Key variations:
> | Delivery | Effect |
> |----------|--------|
> | **Off-break (stock)** | Turns from off to leg (into RHB) |
> | **Arm ball** | Bowled with less spin — goes straight on |
> | **Doosra** | Turns the other way (off to leg reversed); requires legal bowling action |
>
> IPL examples: Ravichandran Ashwin (RCB/CSK/others), Axar Patel (DC — slow left-arm, the left-handed equivalent), Sunil Narine (KKR — mystery off-break variant).

## Required Concepts

- Off spinners and left-arm orthodox spinners (slow left-arm) are "finger spinners" — cheaper in T20 than wrist spinners but less variable turn
- The arm ball (no turn, goes straight) is the primary wicket-taking variation — batter plays for turn that doesn't come, hits stumps
- In T20, off spinners typically operate in the middle overs (7–15) where they can set up batters across multiple deliveries
- Sunil Narine's "mystery spin" is technically an off-break variant with an unusual action that is harder to read

## Citation Behavior

1. Define off spinner as right-arm finger spin, off-to-leg turn (into RHB).
2. Distinguish from leg-spin (opposite turn) and from left-arm orthodox (left-handed equivalent).
3. IPL example: Ashwin is one of the most experienced IPL off-spinners historically.

## Caveats

- "Off spin" and "off-break" are used interchangeably in commentary.
- The doosra is controversial — some bowlers have been reported for illegal bowling actions when attempting it.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "An off spinner turns the ball away from the right-handed batter." *(An off spinner's stock delivery turns INTO the right-handed batter — from off stump toward leg stump. It is a leg spinner who turns the ball AWAY from the right-hander. Reversing this is a fundamental error in cricket terminology.)*
