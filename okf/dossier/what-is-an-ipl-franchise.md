## User Question

> What is an IPL franchise?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> An **IPL franchise** is a privately owned entity that holds the rights to operate a team in the Indian Premier League. Key features:
>
> | Feature | Detail |
> |---------|--------|
> | **Ownership** | Private companies/investors (not BCCI) |
> | **City rights** | Each franchise represents a home city (e.g., Mumbai Indians → Mumbai) |
> | **Player acquisition** | Via IPL Mega Auction or Mini Auction (bid for players' contracts) |
> | **Revenue share** | Franchise earns from merchandise, local sponsors, and a share of BCCI's central pool (broadcast rights) |
> | **Salary cap** | Each franchise operates within a capped player wage budget |
>
> There are **10 franchises** in IPL 2026: CSK, MI, RCB, KKR, DC, GT, LSG, PBKS, RR, SRH.
>
> Franchises retain certain players via "retention" before each Mega Auction, and bid for remaining players during the auction.

## Required Concepts

- A franchise is the commercial entity; the team is the on-field unit. "Mumbai Indians" refers to both the team (players) and the franchise (MI Sports Private Limited)
- Franchise valuations have grown significantly — MI is valued at over $1 billion by some estimates
- Revenue split: BCCI receives the central media rights fees and redistributes a share to franchises; franchises keep local sponsorship and gate receipts
- The 10-team format (since IPL 2022) added GT (Gujarat Titans) and LSG (Lucknow Super Giants)

## Citation Behavior

1. Define IPL franchise as a private ownership entity with city-based team rights.
2. State 10 franchises in IPL 2026 and list them.
3. Note player acquisition via auction (not draft or free agency).

## Caveats

- Unlike some cricket leagues, IPL franchises are permanent (not promoted/relegated) — once granted, franchise rights are held indefinitely unless revoked for regulatory reasons.
- BCCI controls the tournament format, player eligibility, and central contract terms.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "IPL franchises are owned by the BCCI." *(IPL franchises are privately owned — by companies and investors. The BCCI owns and governs the IPL as a tournament but sells franchise rights to private parties. Franchise owners include corporations, film personalities, and conglomerates.)*
