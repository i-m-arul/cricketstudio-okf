## User Question

> What is a Chinaman bowler in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **Chinaman bowler** is a **left-arm wrist spinner** — a bowler who imparts wrist-spin rotation with their left hand.
>
> For a right-handed batter facing a Chinaman:
> - The **stock ball** turns INTO the batter (from off to leg) — opposite to a conventional left-arm spinner
> - The **"wrong-un"** (equivalent of the googly) turns AWAY from the batter
>
> Comparison:
> | Bowler type | Hand | Stock ball turn (vs RHB) |
> |-------------|------|--------------------------|
> | Off-spinner | Right | Off to leg (into RHB) |
> | Left-arm orthodox | Left | Leg to off (away from RHB) |
> | Leg-spinner | Right | Leg to off (away from RHB) |
> | **Chinaman** | **Left** | **Off to leg (into RHB)** |
>
> **Kuldeep Yadav** is the leading Chinaman bowler in IPL — 102 career wickets, 8.08 economy, with 83/102 wickets from middle overs (1818 balls, 8.04 econ).

## Required Concepts

- "Chinaman" is a colloquial cricket term for left-arm wrist spin — entirely a cricket usage, not a modern social context
- The Chinaman's odd turn direction (into right-handers vs the more typical left-arm spinner direction) is what makes it difficult to read
- The bowling action looks similar to left-arm spin but the wrist controls the turn — harder for batters to pick

## Citation Behavior

1. Define Chinaman as left-arm wrist spin, stock ball turning into right-handers.
2. Contrast clearly with left-arm orthodox (arm spin, turns away from right-handers).
3. For Kuldeep Yadav specific data, cite the kuldeep-yadav-ipl-career.md entry.

## Caveats

- "Chinaman" is the accepted cricket term but some broadcasters prefer "left-arm unorthodox spin" or "left-arm wrist spin."

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "A Chinaman bowler turns the ball away from right-handed batters." *(The stock Chinaman delivery turns INTO right-handers (from off to leg). That's the opposite of a conventional left-arm spinner who turns away from right-handers. The Chinaman turns INTO right-handers — same direction as an off-spinner, but bowled with the left hand and wrist action.)*
