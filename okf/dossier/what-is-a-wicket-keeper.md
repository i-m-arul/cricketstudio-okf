## User Question

> What does a wicket-keeper do in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **wicket-keeper** (often abbreviated "WK" or "keeper") is the specialist fielder stationed **directly behind the stumps** at the batting end. Their responsibilities:
>
> 1. **Catch edges**: when a batter edges the ball, the keeper catches it to dismiss the batter "caught behind"
> 2. **Complete stumpings**: if a batter steps out of the crease to play a delivery, the keeper can break the stumps while holding the ball — a stumping dismissal
> 3. **Run-outs from behind the stumps**: completing run-outs when the ball arrives at the keeper's end
> 4. **Bat in the lineup**: wicket-keepers also bat — in T20, the keeper-batter role (e.g. MS Dhoni, Quinton de Kock) is a premium combination
>
> Every cricket team must designate exactly **one** wicket-keeper per match.

## Required Concepts

- **Stumping**: dismissal where the keeper breaks the stumps while the batter is out of their crease (and the ball is not a no-ball)
- **Caught behind**: dismissal where the ball deflects off the bat's edge and the keeper takes the catch
- **Keeper-batter**: a wicket-keeper who also contributes significantly with the bat — highly valued in T20 formats
- **Standing up vs standing back**: keepers stand right behind the stumps for spin bowlers ("standing up"), or deeper for pace bowlers ("standing back")

## Citation Behavior

1. This is a methodology/glossary entry — no specific numbers.
2. When citing wicket-keeper stats for a specific player, use their scorebook entry.
3. Notable IPL keepers: MS Dhoni (CSK), Quinton de Kock (LSG), Rishabh Pant (DC), KL Rahul (LSG).

## Caveats

- A wicket-keeper is distinct from an "extra fielder" — the position is governed by ICC playing conditions; only the designated keeper may stand directly behind the stumps.
- "Keeper-batter" is a T20 premium role but some players are specialist keepers with limited batting value.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "The wicket-keeper doesn't bat." *(All 11 players in a cricket team bat, including the wicket-keeper. Many notable keepers like MS Dhoni are celebrated for their batting as much as their keeping.)*
