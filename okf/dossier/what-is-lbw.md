## User Question

> What is LBW in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> **LBW (Leg Before Wicket)** is a method of dismissal in cricket. A batter is out LBW if:
>
> 1. The ball would have hit the stumps (determined by the umpire or ball-tracking)
> 2. The ball is intercepted by the batter's body (leg, pad, or any body part) — NOT the bat
> 3. The ball pitched in line with the stumps OR on the off-side (certain conditions apply)
>
> Key exceptions that prevent LBW:
> - If the ball pitches outside leg stump, it cannot be LBW (the batter cannot be penalised for blocking leg-side balls)
> - If the batter hits the ball with the bat before it hits the body, it cannot be LBW
>
> In T20 cricket (including IPL), LBW decisions can be reviewed via the Decision Review System (DRS).

## Required Concepts

- **Stumps**: the three vertical posts; if the ball would have hit them, the trajectory is "in line"
- **DRS (Decision Review System)**: ball-tracking technology used to predict ball trajectory for LBW reviews
- **Umpire's call**: in DRS, if the ball is "umpire's call" on impact with the pad or hitting stumps, the original decision stands
- **T20 context**: LBW is less common in T20 than Tests because batters play more aggressively and hit the ball more often

## Citation Behavior

1. Define: interception by body (not bat) of a delivery that would have hit the stumps.
2. Note the key exception: no LBW if ball pitches outside leg stump.
3. For T20/IPL LBW dismissal counts, cite the specific match or season; CricketStudio records dismissal types in ball-by-ball data.

## Caveats

- LBW rules have several nuances (padding up outside off stump, bat-pad simultaneous contact) — the above is the core definition.
- In T20 cricket, batters "pad up" (deliberately block with pads) less often because the game moves fast and the DRS system can spot errors.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "LBW means the ball hit the batsman's leg." *(The ball must ALSO have been going on to hit the stumps. If the ball would have missed the stumps, it's not LBW regardless of pad impact.)*
