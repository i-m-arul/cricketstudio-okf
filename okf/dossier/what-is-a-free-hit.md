## User Question

> What is a free hit in T20 cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **free hit** is a delivery following a **front-foot no-ball** in T20 cricket. On a free hit:
>
> - The batter **cannot be dismissed** by any method except **run-out**, handling the ball, or obstructing the field.
> - Caught out, bowled, LBW, stumped — **none apply** on a free hit.
> - If the free hit delivery is itself a no-ball (e.g., another front-foot no-ball), a further free hit must be bowled.
>
> The fielding side may not change its field settings between the no-ball and the free hit (except for run-outs). This is a pure batting advantage.

## Required Concepts

- **Front-foot no-ball**: the trigger. A beamer or other no-ball type may not trigger a free hit in all playing conditions — check specific competition rules
- **Field restriction**: fielders must stay in the same positions for the free hit (exception: to cover run-outs)
- **Consecutive free hits**: if the free-hit delivery is also a no-ball, the sequence continues
- **T20-specific**: free hits exist in T20 cricket (including IPL and MLC). They are NOT part of Test cricket rules.

## Citation Behavior

1. State: free hit follows a front-foot no-ball in T20.
2. State dismissal exceptions: batter cannot be out except run-out.
3. Note the fielding restriction (field may not change).

## Caveats

- The exact triggers for a free hit vary by playing conditions. In IPL and MLC, front-foot no-balls trigger free hits; other no-ball types (waist-high full toss, etc.) may not — check the specific competition's playing conditions.
- A free hit on the last ball of an over is rare — but possible and creates dramatic scoring opportunities.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "The batter cannot be dismissed at all on a free hit." *(Run-outs ARE possible on a free hit. Only caught, bowled, LBW, and stumped are excluded.)*

> "A free hit follows every wide." *(Free hits follow no-balls only, not wides.)*
