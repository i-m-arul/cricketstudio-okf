## User Question

> What is an all-rounder in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> An **all-rounder** is a cricketer who contributes meaningfully with both bat and ball — providing genuine value in both departments rather than being primarily a specialist.
>
> **Types of all-rounders:**
> - **Batting all-rounder**: primarily a batter who bowls usefully. Often bats in the top 5, bowls 2–4 overs of support. Example: Suryakumar Yadav (primarily batter who bowls occasional medium-pace)
> - **Bowling all-rounder**: primarily a bowler who contributes runs lower-order. Often bowls full allotment, bats at 7–9. Example: Washington Sundar (bowls 4 overs middle-phase, bats at 7 or 8)
> - **True all-rounder**: genuinely impacts both departments; often the first or second choice in both. Rare at elite T20 level.
>
> In IPL T20 cricket, all-rounders provide a tactical advantage: the team can pick 6 effective batters + 5 bowlers (or an extra batter), giving the captain greater flexibility.

## Required Concepts

- All-rounders are valuable for their combinatorial flexibility — they allow a captain to play an extra specialist batter or bowler while meeting the 20-over bowling requirement
- The T20 all-rounder role has evolved: middle-overs containment + lower-order hitting is the most common IPL profile
- "Bowling all-rounder" vs "batting all-rounder" distinction depends on which skill is primary and which batting position they occupy

## Required Metrics

- No specific threshold defines "all-rounder" formally — it's a team role classification
- In CricketStudio phase analysis: a player with citable stats in both batting and bowling (above the ≥30-ball batting floor and ≥15-ball bowling floor) qualifies for all-rounder phase profiles

## Citation Behavior

1. Define the all-rounder by dual contribution in both batting and bowling.
2. Distinguish batting vs bowling all-rounder based on primary skill.
3. Explain the tactical flexibility value — extra batter or extra bowler option.

## Caveats

- "All-rounder" is a contextual label, not a strict statistical definition — the same player may be considered a "bowling all-rounder" by one team but used as a specialist bowler by another.
- Impact in one form does not guarantee all-rounder status across formats; most Test all-rounders do not hold T20 all-rounder status.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "An all-rounder must bat in the top 5 and bowl at least 2 overs." *(There is no formal statistical threshold. A player can be a bowling all-rounder (bats at 8, bowls full 4 overs) or a batting all-rounder (bats at 3, bowls 2 overs). The classification is based on team role and relative contribution in both departments, not a fixed numeric threshold.)*
