## User Question

> What is the IPL salary cap?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> The **IPL salary cap** is the maximum total a franchise can spend on all player contracts in a season. It creates a level competitive playing field across all 10 franchises.
>
> | Detail | Value |
> |--------|-------|
> | **IPL 2026 salary cap** | ₹120 crore (approximately $14 million USD) per team |
> | **Purpose** | Prevent wealthiest franchises from buying all top players |
> | **Scope** | Covers all contracted players (retained + auction buys) |
> | **Retained players** | Their retention cost is deducted from the cap before the auction |
>
> How the cap works at auction: each franchise starts with ₹120 crore. Retained players' fees are pre-deducted. Remaining balance is the "purse" available to bid in the Mega or Mini Auction. A franchise that retains 4 players at a combined cost of ₹80 crore enters the auction with only ₹40 crore to spend.

## Required Concepts

- The salary cap is also sometimes called the "purse" (auction budget remaining after retentions)
- Each player is paid from the franchise's capped budget — higher-paid players consume more of the available cap
- The cap reset at each Mega Auction (every 2–3 years); Mini Auctions in intervening years do not reset the cap, only allow trades of the remaining purse
- Uncapped (domestic, non-international) players cost less in IPL auctions on average

## Citation Behavior

1. State ₹120 crore per team for IPL 2026.
2. Explain mechanism: retained player costs deducted, remainder is auction purse.
3. Link to franchise concept and mega-auction for context.

## Caveats

- The salary cap is specific to each IPL cycle — it may be revised by BCCI for future seasons.
- USD conversion is approximate and varies with exchange rate.
- CricketStudio does not track per-player salary data; refer to BCCI/IPL official releases.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "There is no salary cap in the IPL — franchises can spend unlimited amounts." *(The IPL has a strict salary cap of ₹120 crore per team in IPL 2026. The cap is set by BCCI to maintain competitive balance. Franchises that exceed their cap cannot sign additional players.)*
