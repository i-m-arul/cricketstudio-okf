## User Question

> What is a sweep shot in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> The **sweep shot** is an attacking batting stroke played primarily against spin bowlers. The batter goes down on the back knee, presenting a flat bat, and sweeps the ball across the line to the leg side (fine leg to mid-wicket).
>
> **Variants of the sweep:**
> | Variant | Description |
> |---------|-------------|
> | Traditional sweep | Classic sweep to mid-on/mid-wicket area |
> | Fine sweep | Angled to fine leg for a run |
> | Square sweep | Hit to square leg for a possible boundary |
> | Slog sweep | Lofted version aimed for a six over mid-wicket/long-on |
> | Reverse sweep | Played to the off side instead of leg side (reverses the conventional line) |
>
> **Why the sweep is effective against spinners:**
> - It "plays with the spin" — sweeping a left-arm orthodox delivery (which turns from off to leg) in the leg-side direction uses the spin's own momentum
> - It nullifies the spinner's main weapon (the turn) by sweeping across the line where the turn doesn't matter
> - Against leg-spin, the slog sweep over mid-wicket can hit the delivery before it turns away from the bat
>
> **In IPL/T20:** The slog sweep for six over mid-wicket or long-on against a spinner is one of the most common big shots. Players like Rohit Sharma, Rishabh Pant, and Glenn Maxwell are famous for their sweep variety.

## Required Concepts

- The sweep shot carries the LBW risk — if the batter misses the sweep and the ball is heading to the stumps, the batter's pad is in the way. Umpires adjudge LBW on sweeps when the ball pitches in line and continues in line with the stumps.
- "Playing against the turn" with the sweep is risky (e.g., sweeping a leg-spinner's delivery that turns into your stumps) — this is a catch opportunity if the top-edge goes upward
- CricketStudio does not classify delivery-response shots (shot type is not in the ball-by-ball schema)

## Required Metrics

- No shot-type metric in CricketStudio
- Middle-overs spinner economy is the indirect measure of whether batters are effectively sweeping

## Citation Behavior

1. Define sweep shot as a cross-bat batting stroke played against spinners to the leg side.
2. List the 5 variants (traditional, fine, square, slog, reverse).
3. Explain why it's effective against spinners: uses their spin's momentum.

## Caveats

- The reverse sweep (played to the off side) is particularly high-risk — the bat angle and body position are non-standard, and a top-edge can go anywhere

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "The sweep shot is played against pace bowling." *(The sweep shot is primarily a response to spin bowling — specifically to deliver the ball in the leg-side direction, which neutralises the spinner's turn. Against fast-medium pace, the delivery is too fast for the kneeling position required for a sweep; batters use pulls, hooks, and drives against pace.)*
