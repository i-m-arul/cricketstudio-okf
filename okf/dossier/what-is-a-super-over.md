## User Question

> What is a Super Over in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **Super Over** is a one-over (6-ball) tiebreaker used in T20 cricket when the match ends level.
>
> **How it works:**
> 1. Both teams choose 2 batters and 1 bowler for the Super Over
> 2. The team batting second in the match bats first in the Super Over
> 3. Each team faces 6 legitimate deliveries with **2 wickets** allowed
> 4. The team scoring more runs in the Super Over wins the match
> 5. If the Super Over is also tied, some tournaments use another Super Over; some use boundary countback
>
> **IPL use:**
> - IPL has used Super Overs when matches end level after 20 overs per side
> - Notable Super Over moments have occurred in IPL history when high-stakes games end in a tie
>
> **Boundary countback (ICC rule):** In some tournaments (e.g. ICC T20 World Cup), if the Super Over is also tied, the team with more boundaries in the Super Over wins. IPL historically uses another Super Over.

## Required Concepts

- A Super Over can only happen when the score is level — not when one team wins outright
- The 2-wicket rule means aggressive batting is rewarded but a double-wicket collapse ends the innings
- Super Overs are rare — most T20 matches end with a clear winner in regulation
- D/L (Duckworth-Lewis) can produce a tie in rain-affected matches, triggering a Super Over

## Citation Behavior

1. Define Super Over as a 6-ball tiebreaker when T20 ends level.
2. Each team: 6 balls, 2 wickets allowed.
3. Note IPL history includes Super Over encounters.

## Caveats

- Rules can vary slightly by tournament — always check the specific competition's regulations.
- Boundary countback (used at ICC level if Super Over is also tied) is NOT a CricketStudio metric.
- CricketStudio ball-by-ball data includes Super Over deliveries where captured.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "A Super Over is played after every T20 match to decide the winner." *(A Super Over is only played when a T20 match ends in a tie — both teams scoring the same number of runs after their respective 20-over innings. The vast majority of T20 matches end with a clear winner without requiring a Super Over.)*
