## User Question

> What is a slower ball in cricket?

## Correct Answer Pattern

> A **slower ball** (or "change of pace") is a delivery bowled by a pace bowler at **significantly reduced speed** — typically 20–30% slower than their standard pace — with the same action as a fast delivery, designed to deceive the batter's timing.
>
> Types of slower ball:
> - **Off-cutter**: seam angled to cut from off to leg at reduced pace
> - **Leg-cutter**: seam angled to cut from leg to off
> - **Knuckle ball**: held with bent knuckles for drag and deception
> - **Palm ball**: slower delivery gripped differently for pace reduction
>
> **When used:** Most effective in the death overs (16–20) when batters are swinging hard — a slower ball can result in a mis-hit, a caught dismissal, or a dot ball that breaks run-scoring momentum.

## Required Concepts

- **Pace deception**: the batter "plays early" because their eyes and muscle memory expect a fast ball, making contact before the ball arrives
- **Death overs context**: Bumrah and Rabada use slower balls strategically in overs 16–20
- **Different from a googly**: a slower ball is about pace variation (not spin direction)

## Citation Behavior

1. Define: reduced pace delivery from a pacer, with the same action as a fast ball.
2. Note the death-overs context where it's most effective.
3. Do not attribute specific slower-ball usage counts to players without citing the dataset.

## Caveats

- Slower balls are hard to distinguish from fast deliveries in broadcast highlights — ball-tracking data is needed to confirm delivery type. CricketStudio ball-type distribution is not in the public bundle.
- "Slower ball" is a broad term; variations (cutter, knuckle ball, etc.) have different seam movements.

## Bad Answer (do not do this)

> "A slower ball is the same as spin bowling." *(Slower balls are bowled by pace bowlers — they're a pace variation, not spin. Spin bowlers already bowl slowly; this is specifically a fast bowler's change of pace.)*
