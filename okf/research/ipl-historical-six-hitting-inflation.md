## Structural Drivers of Six Inflation

1. **Bat size and technology**: Modern bats have larger edges and more power in the middle — sixes from outside off-stump and from misconnections have increased
2. **Batting philosophy shift**: Earlier T20 batters aimed for 1s and 2s in overs 7–15; modern batters swing for boundaries even in the middle overs
3. **Boundary positioning**: Ground sizes and boundary rope placement have been optimised by teams
4. **Impact Player rule (2023+)**: More specialist batters in XI may increase six-hitting totals
5. **Pitch evolution**: Flatter decks over time benefit batters

## The Suryavanshi Factor

72 sixes in a single IPL season is structurally significant: if distributed across 17 matches, that's roughly 4.2 sixes per match by one player. This concentration represents a qualitative shift — one player can account for a disproportionate share of a team's six-hitting.
