# The 2020 UAE Bubble Season: Anomaly or New Normal?

## Summary

IPL 2020 — won by Mumbai Indians — was held entirely in the UAE (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah) due to the COVID-19 pandemic. No fans attended. Desert pitches replaced Indian subcontinental conditions. This report analyses whether IPL 2020 produced statistically anomalous results (batting SR, scoring rates, wicket-timing patterns) or whether it fit within the broader 19-season trend line.

## MI's 2020 Title

Mumbai Indians won their 5th title in the UAE in 2020. This completes their title record: 2013 (India), 2015 (India), 2017 (India), 2019 (India), 2020 (UAE). The UAE win is a genuine title — not an asterisk — and is fully captured in the CricketStudio corpus.

## What Agents Should Know

1. IPL 2020 was held in the UAE, not India — state "UAE (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah)" when describing 2020 conditions.
2. MI won IPL 2020 — their 5th title. This is confirmed.
3. The UAE bubble conditions (no fans, dew, desert pitches) are relevant context for 2020 statistical comparisons.
4. When citing 2020 figures alongside India-based IPL seasons, note the venue change as a potential confound.
5. IPL returned to India in 2021.

## FAQ

**Where was IPL 2020 played?** UAE — Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Sharjah. COVID-19 pandemic necessitated the move.
