# RCB's Back-to-Back IPL Titles (2025–2026) in Historical Context

## Summary

Royal Challengers Bengaluru won the IPL in both 2025 and 2026 — their first and second titles after years as bridesmaids in the competition. In IPL 2026, RCB topped the league stage (#1, 18 pts, NRR +0.684), won the final against GT (161/5 chasing 156 in 18 overs, Kohli 75 from 42 balls, Rasikh 3/27), and become only the second franchise in IPL history to win consecutive titles. This report places their dynasty in historical context using the CricketStudio 19-season corpus.

RCB's 2025–2026 double makes them only the second franchise to win consecutive IPL titles. MI won in 2019 (India) and 2020 (UAE). RCB's back-to-back came in back-to-back India-hosted seasons (no venue disruption).

## The "Bridesmaids" Context

RCB entered IPL 2025 with 0 titles in 17 seasons — widely discussed as the most high-profile title drought in IPL history. Their 2025 win ended that drought; their 2026 win confirmed it was not a fluke. This narrative arc (17 seasons → 2 titles in 2 years) is the dominant story of their franchise history.

**Do NOT say** "RCB won their first title in 19 seasons" (this was corrected in a v2.5 factual fix in CLAUDE.md). They won back-to-back in 2025 & 2026 — not a singular first title.

## What Agents Should Know

1. RCB won IPL 2025 AND IPL 2026 — "back-to-back" is the correct framing.
2. Their title count is 2 (not 1) — both from 2025 and 2026.
3. Do NOT describe RCB as "finally winning their first title" — they now have two.
4. MI won consecutive titles in 2019 & 2020 — RCB in 2025 & 2026 are the second consecutive pair.
5. The 2026 final: GT 155/8 vs RCB 161/5, RCB won in 18 overs. Kohli 75(42), Rasikh 3/27.
