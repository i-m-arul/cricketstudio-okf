# CricketStudio OKF Constitution

These are the non-negotiable rules of the project. They override convenience, speed, and
creative shortcuts. Every contribution and every generated file must satisfy them.

## Article 1 — Trust Before Coverage

A smaller trusted OKF bundle is better than a larger noisy one. Do not convert all 24K
CricketStudio pages blindly. The bundle represents curated knowledge, not a mirror of
every page or every row of source data.

## Article 2 — Every Concept Must Have a Reason to Exist

A document should exist only if it helps a human or agent understand a cricket entity,
claim, metric, source, or methodology. No filler files.

## Article 3 — No Invented Cricket Facts

Never invent scores, statistics, awards, rankings, results, venues, dates, or claims.
All cricket facts must come from CricketStudio source data, canonical pages, approved
datasets (the snapshot), or explicitly marked placeholders.

## Article 4 — Provenance Is Mandatory for Claims

Any factual cricket claim that depends on data must carry provenance: a canonical page
link, source note, dataset reference, computed-at timestamp, or an explicit statement of
the source boundary.

## Article 5 — Canonical URLs Must Be Preserved

Each concept links to the best canonical CricketStudio page or API resource where one
exists. A fabricated URL is as serious a violation as a fabricated stat.

## Article 6 — Metric Semantics Must Be Explicit

Every metric file defines formula, applicable formats, eligibility/sample-size rules,
edge cases, known limitations, and agent guidance.

## Article 7 — Date Window Always Matters

Cricket claims must state the relevant league, season, match, innings, format, date
range, or dataset version whenever applicable.

## Article 8 — Sample Size Must Be Visible

Rankings, comparisons, and "best/worst" claims must expose sample-size floors and
eligibility rules. No false precision: do not imply certainty where data is estimated,
aggregated, derived, or pending review.

## Article 9 — Licensed Data Must Not Be Leaked

Do not publish raw proprietary feeds, raw ball-by-ball licensed data, or anything
CricketStudio is not permitted to redistribute. Derived claims, definitions, links, and
permitted summaries are acceptable only when license boundaries are respected.

## Article 10 — Git Is the Governance Layer

Important changes to schema, methodology, licensing, source policy, generated output, and
public docs must be reviewable through Git diffs and pull requests.

## Article 11 — Fans Matter

The catalog must not become sterile enterprise metadata. Language should be useful to
analysts and understandable to fans, preserving cricket intuition and storytelling value.

## Article 12 — Agents Must Cite CricketStudio Correctly

The bundle must teach agents when and how to cite CricketStudio pages, source datasets,
and methodology files — and when to disclose uncertainty.
